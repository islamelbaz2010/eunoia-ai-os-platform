## Battle Cards (Use These at the Booth)

### vs. "We Use WhatsApp for Knowledge Sharing"

> "WhatsApp is where knowledge goes to die. Your policies are buried in chat history. New team members can't search them. The AI can't learn from them. Six months from now you'll have 40,000 messages and not be able to find the check-in policy from last quarter. Eunoia is where knowledge becomes permanent, searchable, and citable."

**Key fact**: WhatsApp has no versioning, no access control, no audit trail, and no AI retrieval. It's a chat tool being used as a knowledge management system because there was no affordable alternative — until now.

---

### vs. HubSpot

> "HubSpot is a great CRM for a B2B SaaS company selling software. It's not built for hotel operations. It can't tell your front desk staff what the check-in procedure is at 2am. Eunoia can. HubSpot for your Starter plan costs $800/month. Eunoia's Starter is $99. That's a car payment difference."

**Key fact**: HubSpot's "AI" (ChatSpot) is a CRM query interface, not a RAG system. It cannot answer a question from your internal documents.

---

### vs. Notion AI

> "Notion AI helps you write documents. Eunoia helps your staff answer questions from your documents. When your front desk manager asks 'What's the VIP guest protocol?' in Notion, it will write you a generic response. Eunoia will cite the exact line from your SOP with a 94% confidence score."

**Key fact**: Notion has no vector search, no RBAC that scales to 50+ employees, and no CRM. It requires Zapier to connect to any pipeline tool.

---

### vs. "We Built Something Internally (Excel, Shared Drive)"

> "The question isn't whether it's possible to build this internally — it obviously is. The question is whether your IT team's time is worth more spent on this than on your core business. Eunoia cost $99/month and was ready in 2 hours. At $25/hour for an IT person, you break even on Starter in 4 hours of their time saved."

---

### vs. "We Already Have a PMS / CRM"

> "What PMS are you using? [Listen]. [PMS] manages reservations and room state. Eunoia manages knowledge and leads. Does your PMS answer 'What's in the halal menu section?' from your actual documents? Does it let you ask in Arabic? Do your front desk staff use it to find policies? These tools don't overlap."

---

### vs. "We'll Build It With ChatGPT"

> "You can absolutely build this. It will take 3-6 months of engineering time, cost $150,000+ in developer salaries, and you'll still need to build a CRM and a team management system on top. Eunoia is ready today for $99/month. What do your engineers do that's more valuable than $99/month?"

## What We're Honest About

We don't hide our gaps. When asked directly:

**"Do you have a mobile app?"** → "Not yet. Dashboard is desktop. Mobile app is on the roadmap for Q4."

**"Can I upload PDFs?"** → "Not yet — text paste only. PDF/Word upload is our top feature request and is in Sprint 2 development."

**"How many customers do you have?"** → "We're launching today. This exhibition is our first commercial push. You'd be a founding customer."

**"Does it integrate with [PMS]?"** → "CSV import/export today. [PMS] integration is on our roadmap. What's your PMS? I'll add it to prioritization."

**"Is this HIPAA compliant?"** → "We don't process patient data. Eunoia is for operational knowledge only — procedures, protocols, staff guides. No PHI ever enters the system."

Honesty at this stage builds more trust than overselling. The product is strong enough to speak for itself.
